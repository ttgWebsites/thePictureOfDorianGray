Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter #1
  • In the London home of the artist Basil Hallward, two men discuss a portrait. Basil is sharing his latest work with his friend Lord Henry Wotton.
  • Lord Henry praises the painting of an extremely handsome young man and attempts to convince his friend to display the painting.
  • The artist’s reluctance is because he feels he has put too much of his own feelings into the painting.
  • The artist had become obsessed with the subject of the painting, Dorian Gray, whom he had met at a party and had since become the artist’s muse.
  • As the two men were conversing, Dorian Gray arrives at the artist’s home. Basil asks his friend Lord Henry not to corrupt the innocent young man, whose pure nature was quite unlike the Lord’s.
  • Chapter #2
  • In person Dorian Gray is as beautiful as his portrait would suggest.
  • The golden-haired youth convinces Lord Henry to stay while he sat for the artist. Basil’s warning against the Lord’s negative influence seems only to increase Dorian’s interest.
  • Lord Henry describes to Dorian the philosophy by which he lives his life. Lord Henry praises the qualities of youth and beauty and had adopted a very self-serving lifestyle.
  • He reminds Dorian of the fleeting nature of youth and beauty and encourages him to take advantage of his.
  • When the painting was finished, the sight of it upset Dorian. Unlike Dorian, the painting would never age and the young man wished it were the other way around.
  • Dorian agrees to accept the painting as a gift and arranges to attend the theater with Lord Henry that evening.
  • Chapter #3-4
  • Lord Henry discusses Dorian Gray’s past with his uncle.
  • He tells Lord Henry of Dorian’s dark past being raised by his cruel uncle after his parents died.
  • Lord Henry finds the story fascinating and becomes increasingly intrigued by Dorian. Not long after, Lord Henry and Dorian were both present at a dinner held by Henry’s aunt.
  • While most of the guests are appalled at Lord Henry’s remarks about living selfishly and seeking out pleasure, Dorian is captivated.
  • The next month Dorian announces to Lord Henry that he has fallen in love with a young actress, Sibyl Vane. They met while Dorian was attempting to discover life in the slums of London.
  • She was entranced by the attentions offered to her by the handsome gentleman.
  • Henry agreed to go with Dorian and Basil to watch Sibyl in Romeo and Juliet.
  • Lord Henry is pleased with the influence he is having upon Dorian Gray. He receives a telegram informing him that Dorian and Sibyl are engaged to be married.
  • Chapter #5-6
  • While the actress is thrilled by her engagement to a wealthy gentleman, her mother and brother are more skeptical about the match.
  • Sibyl’s brother James warns the girl to be wary of Dorian’s intentions.
  • Sibyl is sure of her love but her brother vows to kill Dorian should anything happen to his sister.
  • Lord Henry informs Basil of Dorian’s engagement.
  • Basil is concerned that Dorian is to marry so beneath his class, while Lord Henry is only interested in observing the boy’s actions.
  • Dorian joins the friends and informs them that his love for Sibyl has shown him the error in Lord Henry’s philosophies about the benefits of selfishness. The men travel to the cheap theater where Sibyl performs.
  • Chapter #7-8
  • That night at the theater Sibyl gives an awful performance.
  • Dorian questions her about her it and she informs him that she no longer has an interest in portraying other characters because she is so in love with Dorian in real life.
  • He quickly realizes that it is not Sibyl that he loves, but her acting. He tells Sibyl he never wants to see her again.
  • Dorian wanders the streets for the rest of the night and when he arrives home he realizes that Basil’s portrait of him has changed.
  • Now the portrait has a cruel look in the mouth, reminding Dorian of his wish that the painting change instead of him.
  • The sneer frightens Dorian and he vows to apologize to Sibyl in the morning.
  • When Dorian awakes late the next morning, the change on the painting is obvious.
  • Lord Henry soon arrives and informs Dorian that Sibyl had committed suicide. Neither Lord Henry nor Dorian is upset by the girl’s death and make plans to attend the opera that night.
  • After Lord Henry leaves Dorian accepts the change in that painting and what it means.
  • He resolves to live his life as sinfully as he desires because his portrait will bear the consequences instead of his person. He then joins Lord Henry at the opera.
  • Chapter #9-10
  • When Basil comes to offer his sympathies to Dorian the next day, he is shocked to witness his impassive attitude.
  • Basil blames the undesirable changes in Dorian on Lord Henry. Basil asks to paint Dorian, but is refused.
  • He then asks for Dorian’s portrait so that he can exhibit it and Dorian vehemently refuses, not even allowing him to lift the screen that covers it.
  • Once more Basil asks Dorian to sit for him and Dorian refuses again and Basil leaves. Dorian then decides to hide his portrait.
  • Dorian has the painting covered and moved to the unused schoolroom at the top of his house and decides that as long as no one can see it then it is irrelevant what it looks like.
  • After locking up the painting he goes to his study and finds a package from Lord Henry.
  • It contains a newspaper story about Sibyl’s death and a book.
  • The book is about a Parisian man who devotes his life to pursuing all of the passions and sins that are frowned upon by his generation. The immoral book fascinates Dorian.
  • Chapter #11-12
  • As the years pass Dorian lives according to the book.
  • While there are rumors of his sordid conduct, they are often tempered by Dorian’s flawless and pure appearance.
  • He lives to discover beautiful things and keep up with contemporary theories and sciences.
  • As the differences between his body and his painting continue to increase, Dorian is the only witness to his portrait.
  • At times he is horrified at the sight of the painting and other times he is glad that he does not have to bear the evidence of his sinful life.
  • Basil runs into Dorian on the street when Dorian is 38. Basil mentions that he is about to leave for Paris for 6 months.
  • Basil expresses his concern for Dorian’s conduct and the rumors that people are spreading.
  • He inquires why many of Dorian’s friends have come to ruin and if his soul is truly evil.
  • Dorian offers to reveal to Basil the diary of his life, which should answer all of Basil’s questions.
  • Chapter #13-14
  • Dorian shows Basil his portrait. Basil is horrified by the disfigured portrait, but recognizes it as his own portrait, and questions how it is possible.
  • Dorian reminds Basil of the day he pledged his soul in order remain youthful forever.
  • While Basil begs Dorian to repent, Dorian is overcome with hatred and stabs and kills him. Dorian conceals the murder.
  • The next morning, Dorian calls upon an old friend and blackmails him into destroying Basil’s body.
  • When Dorian goes to cover the painting, he notices that the hand is dripping with blood.
  • Chapter #15-16
  • Dorian attends a dinner party that night.
  • He is bored until he discovers that Lord Henry will attend.
  • Dorian is unable to eat the meal and becomes defensive when Henry inquires as to his whereabouts the previous night.
  • Dorian leaves the party early and once home he destroys Basil’s belongings and heads to an opium den.
  • Dorian hopes to forget his sins in a haze of drugs.
  • A sailor that had overheard people talking to Dorian follows him out into the street as he left the den.
  • The man grabs Dorian from behind and threatens him with a gun because he is James Vane, Sibyl’s brother.
  • James had been searching for Dorian for years hoping to get revenge for his sister’s suicide.
  • Dorian tells him that he cannot be the man who wronged Sibyl because it was 18 years ago and Dorian’s face was that of a twenty-year-old.
  • James is convinced by Dorian’s argument and returns to the opium den.
  • Once their James is told that Dorian has not aged in the past eighteen years and realizes that Dorian is in fact the man that he has been searching for. Outraged at having been tricked by Dorian, James vows to find and kill him.
  • Chapter #17-18
  • The next week, Dorian and some friends have gathered at his country estate. Lord Henry, Dorian, and two friends discuss the importance of beauty and the cause of love.
  • Dorian removes himself from the conversation and faints when he sees the James Vane staring at him through the window.
  • The next day, Dorian is haunted by the image of James’s face and refuses to leave the house.
  • Three days afterward Dorian went hunting with companions. One of the men fires at a hare and the party hears the cry of a man from the bushes.
  • The man has been shot in the chest and dies quickly.
  • Later Dorian inquires about the dead man, thinking he was a servant, only to identify him as James Vane. Dorian is comforted by the death of the man who had sworn to kill him.
  • Chapter #19-20
  • Dorian visits Lord Henry some weeks after the death of James Vane. He announces that he wishes to become a better man.
  • He proves it by telling Lord Henry that he resisted seducing an innkeeper’s daughter recently.
  • Lord Henry does not believe Dorian and changes the subject to the disappearance of Basil Hallward.
  • Dorian asks if Henry would believe that he had killed Basil, Lord Henry replies that murder is too unrefined for Dorian to commit.
  • The two men then discuss the existence of the human soul. Dorian knows that it does in fact exist, but Henry is doubtful.
  • Dorian requests that Henry refrain from giving anyone else the book that he had given Dorian, which had greatly corrupted him. Lord Henry disregards this, as he has everything else Dorian has said this day.
  • At home, Dorian goes to look at his portrait hoping to see a change in it after his decision to improve his life.
  • Upon looking at the painting Dorian realizes that his attempt at redemption was a hypocritical one.
  • He is enraged by this awareness and attempts to destroy the painting by stabbing a knife into it.
  • Dorian’s servants hear his cry from downstairs.
  • They discover the body of an old man stabbed through the heart with a knife lying in front of a perfect portrait of Dorian Gray, beautiful and twenty-years-old.
  • Examining the rings that he wears, the servants identify the old man as Dorian Gray.
